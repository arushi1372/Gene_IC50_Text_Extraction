The aim of the present study was to investigate the influence of long term intravenous administration of naftidrofuryl (Dusodril-Lipha Arzn) twice daily in a dose of 200 mg in continuous, 4-hour infusion in 500 ml 0.9% NaCl to the patients suffering from a peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD) in a clinical condition with special attention paid to transcutaneous partial oxygen pressure measurements (tcPO2) and rheographic parameters.
